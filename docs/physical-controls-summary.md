# Physical Controls Summary (ISO 27002:2022 Theme 7)

This document provides a breakdown of how DesertTech Solutions protects its physical facilities, assets, and personnel from external and environmental hazards.

---

### 7.1 Physical Security Perimeters
*   **Purpose:** To isolate and protect areas containing sensitive data or information assets using structural barriers.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Unauthorized physical entry, malicious damage, or property destruction by external threat actors.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Reinforced exterior masonry walls, concrete anti-ram bollards at main entrances, and security fencing around perimeter utility infrastructure.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Site blueprints detailing perimeter barriers and certified facility boundary photographic logs.

### 7.2 Physical Entry Controls
*   **Purpose:** To ensure only authorized personnel can enter or exit corporate buildings, rooms, and secure spaces.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Tailgating, insider threats, and undocumented or malicious facility infiltration.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Mandatory RFID badge readers at all primary access doors, a manned front reception desk, and electronic turnstiles.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Centralized access control database configuration logs and real-time electronic badge swipe history audits.

### 7.3 Securing Offices, Rooms, and Facilities
*   **Purpose:** To implement physical design security inside facilities to protect sensitive work environments.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Internal industrial espionage, opportunistic equipment theft, or viewing of restricted physical material.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Dividing the floor plan into zoned access layers; all development labs and accounting offices require specific department badge provisioning.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Internal zone maps detailing access requirements per department and physical electronic locking hardware lists.

### 7.4 Physical Security Monitoring
*   **Purpose:** To continuously monitor environments to detect unauthorized physical breaches immediately.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Delayed intrusion detection, structural burglary, and visual gaps in security incident investigation.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** High-definition CCTV cameras covering all interior corridors, entrance points, and egresses, integrated with real-time laser motion sensors.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Camera placement maps, CCTV storage hardware retention schedules, and automated alerting sensor log tests.

### 7.5 Protecting Against Physical and Environmental Threats
*   **Purpose:** To shield hardware and data from fire, flooding, earthquakes, explosions, and electrical volatility.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Catastrophic data center loss, permanent hardware destruction, and long-term operational downtime.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Eco-friendly gaseous clean-agent fire suppression arrays (FM-200) installed in data rooms alongside floor-level water detection sensors.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Annual fire marshal safety certifications, sensor calibrations, and specialized HVAC preventative maintenance logs.

### 7.6 Working in Secure Areas
*   **Purpose:** To establish operational protocols for employees operating inside high-risk or classified business environments.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Intentional or accidental photography, unmonitored insider access, and leakages of sensitive customer screens.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Strict bans on non-certified smart devices, personal cameras, and recording hardware inside the primary server hub and financial processing zones.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Signed workspace policy agreements and physical security patrol audit logs.

### 7.7 Clear Desk and Clear Screen
*   **Purpose:** To eliminate visual and physical data leak vectors from unattended workstations.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Opportunistic shoulder-surfing, credential theft from post-it notes, and unauthorized data reading by third parties.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Enforcement of an automated 5-minute system timeout rule for all laptops, paired with mandatory lockable filing drawer usage for physical files.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Non-compliance citation reports generated from randomized after-hours physical security checks.

### 7.8 Equipment Siting and Protection
*   **Purpose:** To locate and mount technical assets in a manner that protects them from opportunistic tampering and environmental stress.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Unauthorized physical reconfiguration, component theft, and hardware failure due to poor heat dissipation.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** All networking nodes and servers are bolted into enclosed, key-locked Server Racks within an insulated data center space.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Data center cooling and physical layout design documentation, alongside physical server rack key control logs.

### 7.9 Security of Assets Off-Premises
*   **Purpose:** To preserve information security while corporate assets are processed outside organization walls.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Loss or theft of customer data stored on laptops at airports, homes, or hotels.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Enforcement of full-disk hardware encryption (BitLocker/FileVault) on all employee devices before off-premises authorization.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Mobile Device Management (MDM) master reports showing encryption status compliance across 100% of remote laptops.

### 7.10 Storage Media
*   **Purpose:** To control the lifecycle of information storage media from acquisition to decommissioning.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Residual data salvage from discarded hard drives or legacy storage devices.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Secure, locked depository boxes placed in the office for legacy media waiting for automated degaussing or physical shredding.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Chain-of-custody tracking records and signed destruction certificates from our certified electronic recycling vendor.

### 7.11 Supporting Utilities
*   **Purpose:** To ensure that vital utilities (power, water, heating) remain continuous and isolated from disruption.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Spontaneous utility grid drops causing file corruption, head crashes, or system drops.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Redundant diesel generator systems backed by Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) batteries to seamlessly hold corporate workloads during grid drops.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Monthly automated UPS battery load tests and generator fuel reserves inspection logs.

### 7.12 Cabling Security
*   **Purpose:** To shelter power and data transport wires from interception, wiretapping, or vandalism.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Direct network tapping, packet sniffing, or malicious line cutting that compromises business continuity.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Running core backbone cables inside heavy steel structural conduits running exclusively through underground channels or secure dropped ceilings.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Cabling schematics, physical wiring closet audits, and localized patch panel key access control logs.

### 7.13 Equipment Maintenance
*   **Purpose:** To service organizational processing equipment correctly to ensure ongoing availability and integrity.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Core systems failing unexpectedly due to structural or component fatigue.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** Formal maintenance agreements with server manufacturers requiring quarterly component testing and rapid hardware replacement guarantees.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Completed vendor service invoices, hardware patch logs, and system error ticketing records.

### 7.14 Secure Disposal or Re-use of Equipment
*   **Purpose:** To ensure that technical storage systems are completely cleared before disposal or allocation to another user.
*   **Business Risk Addressed:** Data leaks resulting from data remnants remaining on reused corporate storage hardware.
*   **DesertTech Solutions Implementation:** A mandatory software sanitization process using NIST SP 800-88 compliant multi-pass overwrites before recycling any computer asset.
*   **Example Audit Evidence:** Automated system verification wiping logs detailing the specific hard drive serial number and wipe confirmation status.
