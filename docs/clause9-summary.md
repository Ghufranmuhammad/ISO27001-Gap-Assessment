# ISO 27001:2022 Clause 9 Summary — Performance Evaluation

## Clause 9 – Performance Evaluation
Performance Evaluation establishes how an organization systematically monitors, measures, analyzes, and evaluates its information security program to prove its ongoing effectiveness to stakeholders and certification bodies.

---

## 9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis, and Evaluation
### Summary
The organization must determine what needs to be monitored and measured (including specific information security processes and controls), the methods used to ensure valid results, when the monitoring must be performed, and when the results must be analyzed and evaluated.

### DesertTech Solutions Real-World Context
At DesertTech Solutions, the GRC Analyst and IT Manager do not rely on assumptions. They monitor technical controls dynamically by analyzing log sources, automated patch management data, and validation metrics (e.g., verifying that 100% of deployed corporate devices remain encrypted via unified Mobile Device Management tools).

### Core Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
*   **Number of Open High Risks:** Target: `0`. Measures exposure windows on high-priority vulnerabilities.
*   **% of Risks Mitigated:** Target: `>= 85%`. Demonstrates progress in implementing the risk treatment plan.
*   **Number of Information Security Incidents:** Target: Minimal baseline. Evaluates control bypass rates.
*   **Control Effectiveness Score:** Target: `>= 90%`. Calculated during internal verification activities.

---

## 9.2 Internal Audit
### Summary
The organization must conduct internal audits at planned intervals to provide information on whether the ISMS conforms to the organization’s own requirements, the requirements of ISO 27001:2022, and is effectively implemented and maintained.

### DesertTech Solutions Real-World Context
Audits exist because self-policing introduces blind spots. DesertTech Solutions schedules an annual independent internal audit cycle before the formal third-party certification assessment. The GRC Analyst acts as the objective reviewer, evaluating system configurations, policy documentation, and interviewing owners without bias to find hidden gaps.

---

## 9.3 Management Review
### Summary
Top management must review the organization’s ISMS at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness. Inputs include status of previous actions, changes in external/internal issues, feedback on security performance, trends in risks, and opportunities for continual improvement.

### DesertTech Solutions Real-World Context
Management review is where GRC data is translated into business strategy. The CEO, GRC Analyst, and IT Manager meet semi-annually. The GRC team presents the KPI dashboard and audit findings. The CEO uses this performance evaluation data to make critical strategic determinations—such as allocating budget for an automated compliance management tool or hiring dedicated security personnel.
