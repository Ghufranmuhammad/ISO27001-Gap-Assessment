# Visitor Management Procedure

## 1. Visitor Registration & Identity Verification
Upon physical arrival at any DesertTech Solutions facility, all non-employee guests must report to the primary reception desk. The visitor must state the explicit purpose of their visit and name their corporate employee host. The receptionist must verify the visitor's identity by validating a legitimate government-issued photo identification card (Passport, Driver’s License, or National ID).

## 2. Temporary Badges & Escort Requirements
Following identity validation, the guest will register their details on the electronic kiosk to receive a temporary visitor badge. 
*   **Visual Distinction:** Visitor badges feature a high-visibility **Red Border** to differentiate them from blue employee badges instantly.
*   **Escort Constraints:** Visitors must be escorted by their DesertTech employee host at all times while inside the building. At no point may a non-employee walk unescorted past the reception turnstiles.

## 3. Exit Procedure & Badge Return
When the business meeting or maintenance task concludes, the employee host must escort the visitor back to the reception area. 
*   The visitor must return the physical temporary badge directly to the receptionist.
*   The receptionist will close out the visitor session on the tracking log, marking the exact timestamp of departure.
*   Returned temporary badges are audited at the end of every business day to ensure zero guest credentials remain unreturned.

## 4. Visitor Log Retention
All electronic visitor history records (names, corporate affiliations, host names, check-in/out times, and identification validation logs) are archived securely. To comply with ISO 27001 tracking standards and regulatory compliance policies, these system database files are maintained for a minimum of **3 years** before automated deletion.
