## Control: Access Control
* **Why applicable?**
  DesertTech stores customer and employee information. Access Control ensures only authorized users can access sensitive systems.
* **Risk addressed:**
  Unauthorized access to confidential information.
* **Evidence:**
  * Access Control Policy
  * User access review records
  * Microsoft Entra ID or Active Directory reports
  * Access request approvals

## Control: Backup
* **Why applicable?**
  DesertTech relies on critical business, financial, and customer data that must be protected against data loss. Implementing regular backups ensures that operations can continue smoothly in the event of hardware failures, ransomware attacks, or accidental deletion.
* **Risk addressed:**
  Permanent loss of critical data, extended operational downtime, and inability to recover from destructive cyber incidents or ransomware.
* **Evidence:**
  * Information Backup Policy
  * Backup logs and status reports
  * Cloud backup configuration settings
  * Information backup testing and restore test reports

## Control: Security Awareness
* **Why applicable?**
  Employees represent a significant element of DesertTech's security perimeter. Conducting regular training ensures that staff members can recognize threats like phishing, social engineering, and weak passwords, reducing the risk of human error.
* **Risk addressed:**
  Successful phishing attacks, credential harvesting, social engineering scams, and unauthorized data disclosure due to employee negligence or lack of awareness.
* **Evidence:**
  * Security Awareness Training Program / Policy
  * Training completion logs and attendance records
  * Phishing simulation results and reporting metrics
  * Training curriculum/materials and onboarding documentation

## Control: Logging
* **Why applicable?**
  To maintain visibility across DesertTech's digital infrastructure, it is vital to record event logs for user activities, exceptions, and security events. This allows for timely detection of anomalies and retroactive incident analysis.
* **Risk addressed:**
  Undetected malicious activity, lack of visibility during security incidents, and inability to conduct effective forensic investigations following a data breach.
* **Evidence:**
  * Logging and Monitoring Policy
  * Centralized SIEM configuration dashboards and alert records
  * Audit logs from critical domain controllers, firewalls, and cloud applications
  * Log retention and preservation configurations

##  Control: Physical Entry Controls
* **Why applicable?**
  DesertTech's on-premise infrastructure, endpoints, and server rooms house sensitive systems and data repositories. Physical entry controls restrict physical access to authorized personnel only, preventing physical tampering or theft.
* **Risk addressed:**
  Theft of hardware assets, unauthorized physical access to critical network infrastructure, or physical tampering with servers and endpoints.
* **Evidence:**
  * Physical Security and Facility Access Policy
  * Electronic keycard access logs and reports for restricted areas
  * Visitor registration logs and escort records
  * CCTV monitoring logs and facility maintenance records
